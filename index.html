THE DIFFERENCE

Generic validation treats every migration the same. Yours isn't the same.

One-size-fits-all checklists

  • Equal effort spent on problems that never occur and problems that always occur
  • Institutional knowledge lives in the heads of a few senior analysts — and leaves when they do
  • Defects surface late, in parallel runs or after cutover, when they're most expensive and most visible
  • Clients are asked to sign off on assurance, not evidence
  • Lessons from each conversion evaporate; the next project starts from zero

Validation focused where the risk actually is

  • Before any data moves, the system already knows the failure patterns of this specific source-to-target combination
  • Validation effort concentrates on the highest-likelihood, highest-impact risks first — targeted, not generic
  • Issues surface in the first validation cycle, while they're cheap and quiet to fix
  • Clients see an evidence-based risk picture up front, then watch each risk get cleared — to the penny
  • Every confirmed issue and every cleared check is captured, permanently improving the next conversion

THE ENGAGEMENT

How it works on your conversion

1

Profile the migration

Select the source and target. The fingerprint for that combination becomes an instant, evidence-based risk briefing — before any data moves.

2

Focus the validation

Every conversion cycle is checked against the fingerprint, highest-risk patterns first, across plans, participants, balances, loans, and provisions.

3

Prove the reconciliation

Counts and totals verified to the penny at every level, with any exception traced to the exact records involved.

4

Findings carry resolution guidance and an owner; the exception register tracks every item to closure ahead of sign-off.

5

Convert smarter, wave after wave

Confirmed issues sharpen the fingerprint mid-engagement — wave two benefits from wave one, and your next conversion benefits from this one.
